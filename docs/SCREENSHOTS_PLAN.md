# Screenshots Plan

Screenshot concepts for App Store and Google Play listings.

---

## Requirements

| Platform | Sizes Required | Count |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| iOS (6.7") | 1290 x 2796 px | 3-10 screenshots |
| iOS (6.5") | 1242 x 2688 px | 3-10 screenshots |
| iOS (5.5") | 1242 x 2208 px | 3-10 screenshots (optional) |
| iPad (12.9") | 2048 x 2732 px | Optional |
| Google Play | 1080 x 1920 px min | 2-8 screenshots |

Use iPhone 15 Pro Max simulator (6.7") as the primary source, then resize for other slots. Google Play screenshots can use the same images resized.

---

## Screenshot Sequence (8 screenshots)

### 1. Explore Feed (Hero Shot)
**Screen:** Explore tab, list view with several event/activity cards visible
**Setup:**
- Location set to an area with good event density
- "All" kind filter selected
- A mix of events and activities showing cards with images, titles, categories
- Weather indicator visible in header

**Caption:** "Discover events and activities near you"

---

### 2. Map View
**Screen:** Explore tab, map view toggled on
**Setup:**
- Map showing pins for multiple events/activities
- At least one card preview visible at bottom
- Zoom level shows a neighborhood-scale area

**Caption:** "See what's happening on the map"

---

### 3. Event Detail + Friends Going
**Screen:** Event detail page for a populated event
**Setup:**
- Event with a good image, clear title, date, location
- "Going" count visible (e.g., "12 going")
- At least 1-2 friend avatars in the "friends going" section
- RSVP button visible
- Share and directions buttons visible

**Caption:** "See who's going and join them"

---

### 4. Dual Camera Check-In
**Screen:** Camera screen in dual-camera mode or the resulting post preview
**Setup:**
- Back camera showing an event/venue scene
- Front camera inset showing the user's face (use a placeholder face or blur)
- Caption field visible

**Caption:** "Check in with dual-camera photos"

---

### 5. Social Feed
**Screen:** Feed tab with 2-3 friend posts visible
**Setup:**
- Posts showing different photo styles (single photo, dual camera)
- Reaction emojis visible on at least one post
- Comment count visible
- Friend usernames and avatars showing

**Caption:** "See what your friends are up to"

---

### 6. Profile + Stats
**Screen:** Profile tab showing user stats and post grid
**Setup:**
- Avatar, username, bio visible
- XP, streak, and friends count in stats row
- Post grid showing 6+ posts (3x2 minimum)
- Upcoming plans carousel showing 1-2 events

**Caption:** "Track your streak and plans"

---

### 7. Filters & Categories
**Screen:** Explore tab with the Filter Sheet open
**Setup:**
- Show the filter modal with category chips, price selector, time window options
- Demonstrate the range of filtering options available

**Caption:** "Filter by category, price, date, and more"

---

### 8. Dark Mode
**Screen:** Explore tab or Feed tab in dark mode
**Setup:**
- Switch to dark theme in Settings before capturing
- Show a populated screen (explore cards or feed posts)
- Demonstrates the app looks polished in both themes

**Caption:** "Light and dark themes"

---

## Capture Tips

1. **Use a test account with realistic data** — seed friend connections, posts, and RSVPs before capturing
2. **Capture on iOS Simulator** at the exact required resolution (iPhone 15 Pro Max for 6.7")
3. **Use `Cmd+S` in Simulator** to save a screenshot to Desktop
4. **Hide the status bar clock** or set it to a clean time like 9:41 (Apple's marketing standard)
5. **Fill the screen** — scroll to hide empty states, ensure cards have images loaded
6. **No personal data** — use test usernames, not real names
7. **Consistent theme** — use light mode for screenshots 1-7, dark mode only for screenshot 8

---

## Caption Style Guide

- Keep captions short (5-7 words)
- Start with a verb or highlight a benefit
- No exclamation marks or marketing hype
- Match the actual feature shown in the screenshot

---

## Adding Captions to Screenshots

Use a tool like:
- **Figma** — free, create a frame at the device size, add text above the screenshot
- **Screenshots Pro** (macOS) — paid, drag-and-drop caption overlays
- **Hotpot.ai** — free browser-based screenshot framing tool

Place the caption in a colored banner above or below the device frame. Use the app's primary color (`#4A90D9` / Colors.primary) for the background.

## Google Play Feature Graphic

In addition to screenshots, Google Play requires a **Feature Graphic** (1024 x 500 px).

**Concept:** App logo centered on a gradient background matching the app's color scheme. Keep it clean — logo + tagline "Find events. Go with friends." No screenshots in the feature graphic.
